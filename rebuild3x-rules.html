Foundation 01
The system
survives your week.
Most programs fail busy men not because they are weak, but because the system was never built for their life. This one was built for yours.
The one thing to remember
Never miss twice.
Miss a session, come back the next day. Ate badly, start fresh in the morning. One slip is human. Two slips becomes a pattern. You do not restart. You continue. That is the whole difference.
Why three days works
Three sessions done for twelve months always beats six sessions done for six weeks.
My role
I am with you every single day.
I check in, I watch the data, I adjust your plan. But I can only do that if you show up and keep me informed. Everything you log, every update you send, makes your results more precise. DM me any time something feels off.
1
When a session is missed, message me the same day so I know and can adjust the week.
2
When a week feels hard, tell me before it becomes two hard weeks.
3
Never go quiet. The results come from the communication between us, not just the training.
Foundation 02
One number.
Protein.
You are not tracking calories. You are not weighing food. You have one daily target. I set that number based on your weight and your goal. Hit it every day.
Why this number drives everything
Protein keeps you full, protects your muscle, and drives fat loss.
When you consistently hit your protein target, overeating becomes difficult. Your body holds muscle while burning fat. Everything else in your nutrition falls into place around that one number.
1
Open Trainerize after every meal and log what you ate. Takes 60 seconds.
2
Check your protein total at midday. If you are behind, your evening meal closes the gap.
3
If you are consistently missing the target, DM me. I will adjust the number or the structure so it becomes achievable for your schedule.
Log your meals in Trainerize every day. I look at those logs. That is how I know what to adjust and when. Without them I am working blind.
Between meals
Fruit works. Keep it simple.
If you are hungry between meals, fruit is a clean option. It keeps you away from food that works against your deficit. Keep it light and log it.
Foundation 03
Build every
plate the
same way.
No weighing. No counting at the table. Look at your plate and know in two seconds if it is built right. This is what right looks like.
Every meal
Protein — half the plate
Chicken, beef, fish, eggs, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese
Carbohydrate — one quarter
Rice, potatoes, oats, bread, pasta
Vegetables — one quarter
Anything green or colourful
Fats — add where needed
Olive oil, avocado, nuts. Use them, do not build the meal around them
How to protect your deficit at every meal
Protein fills the plate first. Everything else fits around it.
The most common reason progress stalls is people underestimate what they eat. Build the protein portion first, then fill the rest.
1
Put your protein source on the plate before anything else.
2
Fill one quarter with a carb source and one quarter with vegetables.
3
If the plate does not look right, DM me a photo. I will tell you exactly what to adjust.
Foundation 04
Three meals.
No more.
Three meals a day, each built using the plate rule and centred around your protein target. This structure keeps your deficit in check without obsessing over food. Log every meal in Trainerize.
Meal one — morning
Start with protein.
When you start the morning with a solid protein meal, you are far less likely to overeat later. Do not skip this meal thinking it saves calories. It does the opposite. A skipped breakfast almost always leads to overeating at dinner.
Eggs with toast. Greek yogurt with fruit. Oats with a protein source. Aim for 30 to 40 grams of protein.
Meal two — midday
Your biggest meal of the day.
Full plate rule applied. This is where most of your daily protein gets hit. Do not make this meal small. Eating too little here means you will eat too much at dinner and undo the deficit you worked for all day.
Chicken with rice and salad. Ground beef with potatoes and vegetables. Tuna with pasta.
Meal three — evening
Close your protein target.
Check where you are on protein before you cook. This meal fills what is left. Eat to close the target, not to feel stuffed. The evening is where most people overshoot and quietly kill the deficit. Keep portions controlled here.
Salmon with vegetables. Eggs with avocado. Ground beef with salad.
Eating out or travelling? Apply the plate rule visually. Order protein first, fill the rest around it. DM me before if you are unsure and I will tell you exactly what to order.
Foundation 05
Three days.
Show up.
Three sessions per week. Your days are set in Trainerize. I built your program around your schedule. Follow it.
When life gets in the way
Twenty minutes still counts. Never let zero happen.
What every session requires
Follow the program. Log the weights and reps.
Open Trainerize. Do what is written. Log it when you are done. That data is how I make your program more effective every single week. Without it I cannot progress you.
1
Open Trainerize before the session, not when you arrive at the gym. Know what you are doing before you walk in.
2
Log every set as you complete it. Weight used, reps done.
3
If a movement feels wrong or causes pain, stop and DM me immediately. Do not push through something that does not feel right.
Rest days
Move, do not train.
Walk, stretch, hit your steps. Your body rebuilds on rest days. That is when the work you did in the gym actually becomes muscle.
Foundation 06
Outside
the gym.
Three habits that run in the background every day and account for more of your results than most people realise.
Daily movement
6,000 to 10,000 steps a day.
Not a workout. Your daily baseline. Over months this adds up to more than most gym sessions. Aim for 6,000 to 10,000 every day.
1
Check your step count at 8pm every evening. If you are under 6,000, go for a 15-minute walk before bed.
2
Park further away, take stairs, walk on calls. These small decisions compound over months.
Sleep
Seven to eight hours. Protect it like a training day.
Without sleep your hunger spikes, your recovery drops, and your training produces almost nothing. You cannot out-train bad sleep. It is a requirement, not a preference.
Water
2.5 to 3 litres a day.
Dehydration makes you feel tired and hungry when you are not. Start every morning with a full glass before coffee. Carry a bottle. Drink consistently through the day.
Every Sunday morning, step on the scale. Same time, before eating. Log the number in Trainerize. I track the trend week by week and adjust your plan based on what I see.
Progress
How we
track results.
Progress is not something you feel week to week. It is something you measure. I watch the numbers and adjust the plan. Your job is to give me accurate data to work with.
What I track
Your weight trend, your logs, your honest updates.
Your weekly weight, your meal logs, your training data. All of that feeds into every decision I make about your program. The more consistent you are, the more precise I can be with your results.
What the scale actually means
The direction over four weeks is what matters.
Daily weight fluctuates and that is completely normal. I look at the trend across weeks, not any single number. If the trend is not moving the right way, I adjust the plan. That is my job.
Most important
A bad week you tell me about is a problem I can fix. A bad week you hide compounds.
When progress slows
It means something needs adjusting. DM me.
Slow progress is never a reason to quit. It is information I use to make the plan better. Keep showing up. Keep the data honest. I handle the rest.
